#Sapients/Noggon
Honorable rabbit ninja villagers who hunt sea creatures.

Most live in [[Emer]], a vast marshland by the Great Sea. #Eo/Emer
Those who live outside the marshlands are often hired mercenaries or spies; Noggon are agile and skilled acrobats, allowing them to access places they aren't authorized.

Noggon are great woodworkers. They can shape the wood native to their homeland incredibly well and can work wood to be wrought. Wrought wood is incredibly tough material used for strongholds and sharp weapons. It is comparable to steel in strength, but it is much better for building as it is cheap and can be permanently set after it is shaped.

Noggon know the sea and winds incredibly well, as their peninsula is home to many migrating types of sea creatures. They must know the migration patterns of these animals because a significant portion of their food comes from the ocean.
Squads of Noggits will go out on fishing boats into the shallow sea every day equipped with special spears and movement gear.
	Their spears are composed of wrought wood and they serve as both a fishing weapon and a bendable stick to perform acrobatic movements.
	They take special throwables called Mireglass which can stun animals briefly to make hunting easier.
	They also take ropestring and climbing gear with them to aid their movement on the sea. It's not easy to climb the spires, and it's especially difficult to get all the way up to a floating island.

The Noggon's architecture are something like southeastern asia. They build junk ship fleets.

#Fungus/Rope
Noggon keep a special fungus called Rope which can be harvested for their strong and unusually large fabric-like mycelium. The mycelium, ropestring, can be used to make rudimentary clothing, but most importantly, durable string to climb with or hold things together.

#Eo/Rhwnych 
Noggon occasionally travel to the nearby mudlands to harvest crystals to build more advanced weaponry. The [[Mogs]] do not appreciate this and often retaliate with small raiding parties to try and steal magical artifacts (and whoever owns them).
