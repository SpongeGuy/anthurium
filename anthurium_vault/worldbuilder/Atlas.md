The Great [[Aether]] Shard [[Calamas]] sticks out of the earth here, making this one of three other spots on the planet where the shard can diffuse its immense energy.
Strange things are common here, including otherworldly beings, alternate spaces, and nonsensical geometry. The weather changes drastically here due to the extraordinary amount of aether concentration.
> Every time the weather changes, the flora and fauna are additionally changed to adapt to the new conditions.

#Eo/Atlas/Foedd
The main feature of Atlas is its tallest mountain peak Foedd. The mountain is split in half by Calamas, an endless, oddly pulsating cavern almost seems to replace what would have been the rest of the matter of the mountain.

A cave exists on Atlas which heads directly into the heart of Foedd. Going deeper into the cave makes it evident that the mountain's interior is incredibly bizarre; the caves opens up into a chasm which is infinitely tall, going up and down forever. Ancient mechanisms and buildings are present inside the chasm, some clearly purposed to study the island's properties, and others for darker purposes. The strangest beings live inside the chasm (including a wererabbit).

Every once in a while, Foedd erupts (an overwhelmingly powerful event). When this happens, a monolithic, concentrated beam of aether is shot out into the sky, dispersing out into the area. When this happens, Atlas transforms, its weather and climate changing (as well as its flora and fauna which live on the island).

# Story
The earth, at its creation, had an extreme amount of liquid energy called aether flowing through its core. As a result, the energy compounded upon itself, making the planet swell. Eventually, the crust burst, giving way to four great ethereal chasms around the world, venting the energy into outer space. A thousand years later, the earth's raw flow of energy had diminished and three of the chasms collapsed in on themselves as there was a weakened stream of ether pouring from them. Only one such chasm is still active, mostly forgotten as myth or legend.

 > *The chasm is a rip through four spatial dimensions, as only three spatial dimensions was not enough room for the vast amounts of energy outputted by the earth.*

The final primordial chasm is present on an island known by many names, but it was called Lashu by those who last inhabited the island. It is a remote volcanic island, so remote that its own unique ecosystem was able to build and thrive there. A long time ago it was inhabited by spiritual, cult-like mages. Later on, it was inhabited by a team of scientists. Both groups of people were dedicated to unlocking the secrets of the mountain and learn more about the power of the earth.

The chasm rips through a mountain called Feodd. The mountain is adjacent to several structures built by the two different peoples. Additionally, there are a few passages throughout the mountain's vicinity leading to different areas.

## The Mages (A1592)
#Sapients/Noggon
The mages were initially a group of 12 humanoid beings who had all previously lived in a large village together. The village was quite cozy, nestled in a valley on the mainland.

One day a traveller came over the mountain and into the valley, preaching about dark magic, godlike power, and demons from beyond space's limit. During his stay in the village, he spoke about the power which resided in the earth itself, and how that was a gateway to even greater things.

A few young and curious villagers had opportunities to speak with the traveller late at night, at the inn where he stayed. The traveller gave them all of his greatest artifacts and filled their minds with the promise of power and destiny as long as they were determined and willing to travel a great distance to the hidden island Atlas.

The traveller performed strange, dark magic tricks which baffled the minds of the older villagers, frightening them. Eventually, enough villagers claimed that the traveller was a demon himself and was there to corrupt the minds of the village, they publicly executed him.

After the traveller's execution, those who were touched by his promises gathered enough supplies and set off for the island, having received most of the traveller's possessions before the execution.
