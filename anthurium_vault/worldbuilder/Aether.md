#Aether
A strange interdimensional ethereal substance present from the beginning of time. It is never permanent in reality, existing between dimensions. So it is never completely realized within the world. It is ancient and fundamental, but transient and unstable.
Aether takes on a whole bunch of different forms, but if an object seems magical, chances are it uses aether in some way.

> *Aether is the echo of creation; the fading breath of a god upon the glass of reality.*

#Eo #Aether/Calamas
Aether is not originally from [[Meatspace]], originating from beyond [[Veil|the Veil]]. During [[Beardo|Beardo's]] capture in [[Timeline#^1d3512|A800]], living shards of aether crashed through Meatspace. One, [[Calamas|Calamas Jinn-Aetu]], crashed into Eo but was embedded into it. This means that Eo is an especially magical and chaotic place, allowing for lots of different types of lifeforms to live and prosper.

Aether possesses innate energy which can be directed, molded, and exploited in certain ways given the proper tools or spirit. However, it is incredibly difficult to harness aether as a source of power or to create something new with it. Aether will naturally have its effects on the earth, so it is much easier to collect and harvest byproducts formed by the earth and aether meeting.

Four laws describe the effects of aether on an environment. These have been discovered, forgotten, and rediscovered over ages by scientists and wizards.
### Exposure Gradient
Every effect aether has on its environment is proportional to exposure. Density and duration are both factors.
### Domain Imprinting
Aether naturally leaves evidence on any landscape that it persists in. 
A few examples:
	Glowing cracks in rocks, strange geological growths, unnatural terrain features, floating islands.
	Trees with magic berries, odd mushrooms.
	Animals with unusual adaptations.
### Resonance Vulnerability
All living beings have a "resonance threshold." Any exposure over this threshold risks phase shifting appropriate to the creature's biology and/or culture.

> How do societies see aether?

Aether is mostly celebrated among societies on Eo, as it is the gateway to using magic and creating technology which could never be possible without it.
Some treat it as a sacred concept.
Some covet it.
Some believe that it is accursed and should never be accessed.

> How is aether used to make magic?

Aether is the mechanism which sorcerers can use to perform spells. Aether acts as a sort of medium for supernatural behavior to exist. Therefore, it is much easier to perform magic using tools which access the aether rather than pulling energy from the aether with one's own flesh.
True aether sorcery (using aether directly, rather than using the latent energies aether produces to perform magic/miracles) is incredibly, almost impossibly difficult and exceedingly rare (because it is overpowered and is basically godlike power).
Using the body to manipulate aether for too long without practice has the effect of *fraying* a sorcerer's body; an effect which renders parts of the sorcerer phased out of reality. This effect reverses/heals over time.

#Aether/Veil
The [[Veil]] is the barrier between [[Meatspace]] and the rest of the universe. This barrier keeps out the denser, incomprehensible layers of space so that creatures can live peacefully within Meatspace. It was created at the beginning of time by [[Beardo]], an ancient missing deity, and has nearly limitless energy. 
It is globally forbidden to tap into the energy of the veil because it corrupts the mind of the user and usually unleashes dark, cosmic, unnatural things into the world.
